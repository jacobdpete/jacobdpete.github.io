This website is for a worldbuilding project centering on a country called the [[Rosen Commonwealth]]. This project is takes a lot of inspiration from Hobart Phillips' Vekllei (Link: [MillMint](https://millmint.net/)), which was the inspiration for the central country of this project - the Rosen Commonwealth. It's much higher-quality than mine and very impressive, so I would advise you check it out for a full understanding of what I try to accomplish with this project. 
This website is made possible by Obsidian (Link: [Obsidian](https://obsidian.md/)) and Quartz 4 (Link: [Quartz](https://quartz.jzhao.xyz/)).

This note has been rewritten several times and I am sure it will continue to be, but the Avaya Project, and its focus, the Rosen Commonwealth, envisions an imperfect kind of utopia, imagining a future world that can be both beautifully humane and distressingly unfair. Running through the core of the Commonwealth is its ideological dilemma: whether it sticks to its socialistic principles or moves pragmatically in an increasingly dangerous world.

The utopia on the inside of the Commonwealth is an escape for me and my ideas. The outside world and the Commonwealth's previous iterations function as some kind of critique, (typical in the leftist tradition), of other kinds of communism and capitalism. These narrative components reflect my dissatisfaction with late-stage capitalism and the hyper-consumerist world we live in today.

I try to cover as many aspects of the Commonwealth as I can. Like ideology, elements of the Commonwealth reflect things I'm interested in, as demonstrated by the amount of work that goes into rail transport or urban development. Similarly, there's no cohesive storyline or protagonist in this project; the scale is too big to focus on just one person. However, I do include several characters in my work that serve to develop something more human-scale.

This project serves to legitimize worldbuilding as a hobby to myself, and to share what I put so much time into.
